Computing torques to compensate gravity in humanoid robots

This is a guest post from Grégoire Passault. Check out Gregwar's blog for more robotics posts!

As opposed to robotic arms, humanoid robots are mobile and therefore their contact points with the environment should be accounted for when computing their dynamics.

Here, we derive a way to compute the required torque on a humanoid robot standing on either one or two legs to sustain gravity.
